I am a finance and strategy professional with over 20 years of experience spanning corporate finance, public economics, and strategic advisory. Currently pursuing an Executive MBA at London Business School, I hold a PhD in Economics from Cairo University.

At Ezz Steel — the leading steel producer in the MENA region — I have led transformative transactions including a $300M offshore syndicated financing, a $125M minority stake acquisition, and complex share-swap structures that reshaped the Group's ownership architecture across the region.

Beyond the corporate world, I have advised the Egyptian Parliament, the UN, the IMF, and governments across the Middle East on fiscal policy, public finance, and budget reform — bringing a uniquely broad perspective to every engagement.

Group Corporate Finance Executive Manager 2019 – Present
Ezz Steel · MENA's Leading Steel Producer

As Group Corporate Finance Executive Manager, I hold a pivotal role in Ezz Steel's second phase of regional expansion — leading the Group's most complex strategic transactions, including M&A mandates, share-swap structures, corporate valuations, and the full spectrum of capital markets activity across the MENA region.

My mandate encompasses Financial Planning & Analysis, investment evaluation, and financial modelling — translating multi-layered data into projections and recommendations presented directly to executive leadership. I collaborate closely with Group management on long-term strategic planning, championing initiatives that drive financial growth and operational scalability.

A defining dimension of the role has been the restructuring of the Group's ownership architecture — shifting from an indirect to a direct Ultimate Beneficiary Owner structure — alongside the origination and execution of offshore syndicated financing facilities that have materially transformed the Group's capital structure and regional footprint.

Landmark Transactions
Led acquisition of Al Ezz Flat Steel and Al Ezz Rolling Mills via a non-cash share-swap, converting outstanding debt into equity.
Structured a $125M offshore syndicated facility enabling full acquisition of Al Ezz Dekeila Steel (EZDK) through purchase of a 31% minority government-held stake.
Structured and secured a $300M offshore syndicated financing to acquire all remaining Ezz Steel minority shares listed on the EGX and as GDRs on the LSE.
Oversaw acquisition of an 18% stake in a local steel producer with share valuation in compliance with Financial Regulatory Authority (FRA) regulations.
Senior Economist 2004 – 2018
Ezz Steel

Over a fourteen-year tenure, I established Ezz Steel's in-house capacity for macroeconomic intelligence and strategic economic research — producing the analytical foundation upon which key commercial and regulatory decisions were made. This encompassed sector-specific economic analysis, trend forecasting, and interpretation of market developments that informed leadership at the highest levels.

I served as the internal authority on corporate law, regulatory compliance, and legislative frameworks, maintaining the organisation's understanding of evolving rules affecting the business environment. In parallel, I led cross-functional work spanning Corporate Governance, CSR, and Business Process Management — including deployment of SIPOC methodology for process optimisation across operating units.

Financial & Economic Analyst 2004 – 2011
Egyptian Parliament · Plan and Budget Committee

As Financial and Economic Analyst to the Plan and Budget Committee of the Egyptian People's Assembly, I was responsible for producing the analytical outputs that informed parliamentary oversight of Egypt's fiscal architecture — encompassing technical reports on Public Economic Authorities, State-Owned Enterprises, and macroeconomic policy prepared for senior legislators.

A landmark contribution was the development of the analytical framework underpinning Egypt's transition from line-item budgeting to Performance-Based Budgeting (PBB), as well as alignment of Egypt's state budget classification system with IMF international standards. My research covered government finance statistics, public expenditure, pension systems, and public-private partnerships.

International Advisory & Consultancy 2006 – 2014
UN Women · IMF · Inter-Parliamentary Union · Multiple Governments

Across an eight-year international advisory career, I delivered technical expertise to governments, international organisations, and parliamentary bodies across the Middle East and Africa — spanning fiscal reform, budget modernisation, and socioeconomic policy analysis.

Key engagements included the alignment of the Omani State Budget with the IMF's GFS 2001 framework, technical support to the UN Women initiative to modernise Iraq's State Budget, and an Affordability Assessment for Egypt's water and sanitation sector. I also contributed to foundational work in Gender-Responsive Budgeting — including Egypt's first report on budgeting for children's rights — and delivered parliamentary budget training in collaboration with the Inter-Parliamentary Union.
